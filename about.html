Since 1987

A house, not a hotel chain

Meridian House began as a single stone building on a quiet stretch of Atlantic coastline, bought by the Hale family with the idea that a hotel could feel like a well-kept home rather than a transaction. Four decades later it's grown to 42 rooms across three buildings, but the instinct hasn't changed: fewer rooms, longer relationships, and a staff that remembers your name by the second morning.

We're still independently owned. No loyalty points, no corporate playbook — just a place that gets better the more it's looked after.

Four Decades

How we got here

1987

The original house opens

The Hale family converts a single stone residence into an eight-room inn.

1998

The garden wing is added

A second building doubles capacity and introduces the kitchen garden that still supplies Salt today.

2009

Salt opens to the public

What began as a guests-only dining room becomes a destination restaurant in its own right.

2017

The Horizon Wing & spa

A third building brings the Horizon Suites, the Meridian Penthouse, and the Salt Water Spa.

2026

Still independently owned

Three generations of the Hale family remain involved in day-to-day operations.
